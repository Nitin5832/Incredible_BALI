About Bali —
Island of the Gods

Discover the history, culture and natural wonders that make Bali one of the world's most extraordinary destinations.

The Island

A Unique Hindu Island

Bali is one of over 17,500 islands that make up Indonesia — and arguably the most famous. It is the only island in Indonesia that is predominantly Hindu, giving it a culture and spiritual life entirely distinct from the rest of the country.

Everywhere you look, spiritual life is visible — from the tiny daily canang sari flower offerings placed on doorsteps each morning, to magnificent temple complexes that punctuate the landscape.

The island covers just 5,780 km² but packs in volcanoes, dense jungle, terraced rice paddies, pristine beaches and coral-ringed offshore islands — all within an hour's drive of each other.

Culture & Religion

Balinese Culture

A living, breathing culture expressed through art, dance, ceremony and daily devotion — Bali's unique Hindu identity shapes every aspect of island life.

⛩️
Hinduism & Temples

With over 20,000 temples, faith permeates every village. Besakih on Mount Agung is Bali's holiest site.

🎭
Kecak & Traditional Dance

UNESCO-listed Balinese dance. The Kecak fire dance at Uluwatu at sunset is truly unforgettable.

🌸
Canang Sari Offerings

Each morning, Balinese women prepare small palm-leaf offerings — a daily act of gratitude to the Hindu gods.

🤫
Nyepi — Day of Silence

A full day of silence to mark the Balinese New Year — no flights, no traffic, no lights. Truly unique.
